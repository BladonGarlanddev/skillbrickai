# SkillBrick AI — Design Brief

This document is the seeding brief provided to Figma for the initial design of SkillBrick AI.

---

## Concept

SkillBrick AI is a community library for AI skills — downloadable prompts that make AI agents smarter in specific domains. Think of it as a living library where humans deposit accumulated knowledge so that any AI, anywhere, can draw from it.

The name and concept are rooted in a simple observation: knowledge transfer has always been humanity's greatest challenge. We told stories, then wrote them down, then printed them at scale, then put them on the internet. AI is the next bridge — and SkillBrick AI is the place where individual knowledge becomes collective knowledge.

---

## Visual Direction

The website should feel like a well-designed document. Think Google Docs meets a beautiful whitepaper — clean, readable, unhurried. Not a SaaS dashboard. Not a dark terminal. A place you'd feel comfortable reading slowly.

- **Light themed throughout.** White or near-white backgrounds. No dark mode.
- **Typography-first.** Generous line height, clear hierarchy, content breathes. The skill content itself is the product — let it show.
- **Minimal UI chrome.** Borders are light or absent. Shadows are subtle, like paper resting on a surface. No heavy cards, gradients, or noise.
- **Warm but not cozy.** Clean like Notion or Linear, approachable like a well-designed book, not sterile like a hospital.

---

## Brand Motif — Connected Nodes

The hero and brand identity should incorporate the visual language of connected nodes — a knowledge graph — but rendered softly. Not a sci-fi network diagram. Think: a constellation of ideas on white paper, connected by fine pencil lines.

**Nodes** represent knowledge created through human and AI collaboration — a person working with an AI to distill expertise into a skill.

**Edges** represent the transmission of that knowledge between AI systems. SkillBrick AI *is* the graph — it's the connective tissue that makes isolated knowledge transferable.

The motif should feel like something between a neural network and a library's card catalog — organic but structured, human but precise.

This motif can appear:
- As a hero illustration or background element on the landing page
- Subtly in empty states or section dividers
- As the basis of the logo mark

---

## Key Pages

### 1. Landing / Homepage
- Hero: the philosophical framing ("a library for AI") + connected nodes motif + clear CTA to browse or contribute
- Trending or featured skills — displayed like document titles in a library catalog
- Domain categories (frontend design, writing, research, code review, etc.)
- Brief "how it works" — browse, copy, paste into your agent

### 2. Browse / Discover
- Search bar prominent at the top
- Filter by domain/tags, sort by trending/newest/most used
- Skill cards should feel like entries in a catalog — title, one-line description, author, tag chips
- Approachable enough that a non-technical person browsing for "something to make my AI better at writing emails" feels at home

### 3. Skill Detail Page *(most important page)*
- Renders the skill like a document — readable, clean, content is front and center
- Sidebar or header area with metadata: author, tags, tested on (model), downloads/uses, related skills
- Single clear action: Copy or Download
- Related skills section at the bottom — using the connected nodes motif lightly

### 4. Creator Profile
- Published skills listed like a personal library or bibliography
- Simple bio, total contribution count

### 5. Submit a Skill
- Simple, unintimidating. A text area that feels like opening a new document.
- Frontmatter fields (name, description, tags) as a clean form above the body

---

## Feeling to Avoid

- Dark, techy, or hacker aesthetic
- Heavy SaaS dashboard energy (lots of panels, charts, complex nav)
- Overly playful or startup-y (big gradients, emoji-heavy, neon)
- Anything that would make a non-technical person feel like they've walked into the wrong room

---

## Reference Points

- Notion (document-first, clean, readable)
- Are.na (calm, curatorial, community-focused)
- Stripe Docs (typography, whitespace, clarity)
- A library card catalog (metaphorically — structure, discoverability, authored entries)
